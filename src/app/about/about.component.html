Temple Legend

The temple where it is situated now, was owned by 2 illam's (ancestral home of the Nambudiries) about 4 centuries ago. The temple at that time was in the form of a straw hut. The whole land that was part of the temple were in many acres.

Legend

Aithihyamala (the Garland of Legends), says that Lord Prashurama (the 6'th incarnation of Lord Vishnu) created 108 Shivalayas, 108 Durgalayas and 108 Kalaris, along with temples for Serpents, and brought Nambudiris from outside Kerala (no Nambudiri priests were present in Kerala during the time) to perform rituals in these temples. It is said that Sri Kalarikkal Bhagavathy temple was one of the Durgalayas that Lord Parashurama created.

British Invasion

During the British rule, when Brahmins became the target of persecution, people of these 2 illam's gave away one portion of this land to a Musaliar. The other portion, were the temple was located, were given to a Panikkar family, as they hoped for this temple to be protected and daily rituals performed. VenuGopala Panikkar belongs to this lineage.

After buying this land, many ill's came upon the Musaliar family and stories are abound. Many generations hence, the globe trotting people of this family was told by some visionary, to contact VenuGopala Panikkar, the renowned Astrologer administering the temple now, to get rid of these ill's that had befallen them. Astrologer VenuGopala Panikkar gave advice to do Vedic rituals to get rid of their ill's. They readily consented as they had already lost many lands, many had died due to mysterious diseases and many were having diseases of unknown nature and were in utter poverty.

Ancestry

Venu's great grandmother used to get 4 anas (25 paise - imagine it's value some 3 generations back!!) every day, while sweeping the temple premise. Thus, the family became rich in unimaginable proportions, having huge houses and property. But slowly they lost interest in rituals, due to immense wealth and partly due to British colonization and the onslaught of missionaries.

Needless to say, the Panikkar family started it's decline. Great grandfather started selling the land belonging to the temple, some close relative even sold some divine belongings of the deity, and the family fell into poverty and disease, continuing the trend down to the next generation. The abandonment of the temple continued till the 80's.

VenuGopala Panikkar

After completed basic schooling, VenuGopala Panikkar started learning Astrology under the guidance of his uncle, who was a practitioner. He studied the sacred science for about 9 years. He took pains to learn this sacred science while his family was in poverty.

During this time, his Father died. Within a short span, his 2 brothers also died. This pain led him to enquire about the cause of these incidents and set about to meet renowned astrologers of that time. But he was dissatisfied by the answers he received.

This led him to learn Vedic rituals and Prashna thoroughly. After completing this, he used his learning to understand the mysteries surrounding the temple and his ancestors. He thus started to revive the dilapidated temple.

This was a risky endeavor as whoever tried to revive the temple met with his death immediately. He understood that this temple not only had a single main deity but a host of other beings who had equal importance. He understood the meaning of all this through Prashna, and that each deity represented a planet and has to do rituals accordingly. This fact was not known and was the cause of untoward incidences to those who tried to revive this temple.

Present day

By that time VenuGopala Panikkar got married. In those difficult times, he bought 2 grams of gold, created the Sree Chakra, and asked his Father-in-law, who was also adept in rituals, to do the Prathishta of the Devi. This was done on the star 'Thiruvonam' (Shravana) in the month of 'Edavam' (Taurus) in 1999. There was only a make shift roof made of tarpaulin to protect the deity from the seasons. VenuGopal was 38 years of age then.

During the prathistha day, a girl child came to see VenuGopala Panikkar. As he was busy with the temple rituals, the child asked his wife’s sister of her intention to meet him but everybody got busy with the ritual and soon forgot about her. People believe that it was the Devi herself who came.

Since then, the temple has grown inorganically, people from all walks, flock to receive her blessings. VenuGopala Panikkar is now a renowned astrologer who has made a name in the field of Astrology and Vedic Rituals. People from all over the world consult with him and prosper, taking his advice and Devi's blessings.
